The Nigerian Tomato Stew

About Tomato Stew

Rice is unarguably a staple food in Nigeria. The average Nigerian eats a rice meal at least once a week. In Nigeria, rice can be prepared and served in a variety of ways. One of such varieties is rice and tomato stew. A lot of Nigerian kids grew up enjoying this meal every Sunday.

It is not quite clear, the exact origin of the tomato stew; and if it happens that it is not originally from Nigeria, we surely have made it our food in our own special way - the Nigerian tomato stew.

One of the best things about the tomato stew is its versatility. It can be served with a range of accompaniments such as: white rice, boiled/fried yam, boiled/fried plantains, boiled/fried potatoes, Nigerian Jollof Rice, Spaghetti, Macaroni and so on.

Recipe

For 6 servings
Ingredients Quantity
Fresh, plum red tomatoes 5
Red bell peppers 2
Red onions 1
Habanero peppers 2
Garlic 6 cloves
Tomato paste 12 oz (340g)
Black pepper 1½ teaspoons
Vegetable oil 1 cup (240ml)
Flank steak 1½ lb (680g)
Fresh rosemary 1 teaspoon
Paprika 1 teaspoon
Curry powder 1 teaspoon
Seasoning cubes 2 cubes
Bay leaf 1
Salt To taste

How to prepare the Nigerian tomato stew

  • In a food processor or blender, combine 4 chopped plum tomatoes, red bell peppers, chopped red onion, habanero peppers, garlic cloves, tomato paste, 1 tablespoon salt, 1 teaspoon pepper, and water. Puree until smooth.
  • Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium-high heat.
  • Add the remaining tomato, sliced onion, remaining salt, and remaining pepper. Sauté until fragrant.
  • Add steak, rosemary, paprika, curry powder, and bullion cubes. Cook until the steak is golden brown and cooked through, about 10 minutes.
  • Add the blended tomato and pepper mixture and the bay leaf.
  • Bring to a boil over high heat. Cover and simmer at low heat for 20 minutes, until the stew has thickened.
  • Fish out the bay leaf.
  • Remove stew from heat and let sit 10 minutes.
  • Serve with white rice and fried plantains. Garnish with scallions and parsley, if desired.
  • Enjoy!
